This document appears to be the minutes of a meeting of the President's Foreign Intelligence Advisory Board (PFIAB) held on December 27-28, 1962. The meeting focused primarily on the U.S. intelligence community's activities related to the Soviet military buildup in Cuba, as well as general intelligence regarding Cuba, China, and the USSR. The document includes discussions of a draft report on intelligence community activities related to the Cuban arms buildup, the status of implementation of prior-Board recommendations, the MONGOOSE activity, intelligence collection efforts with respect to Cuba, and the use of Guantanamo Naval Base for intelligence operations. It also touches on discussions with key intelligence officials, including the Director of Central Intelligence (DCI) John McCone, regarding the intelligence handling of the Cuban crisis and concerns about potential intelligence let-downs.
