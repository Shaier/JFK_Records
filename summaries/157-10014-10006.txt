This document is a transcript of a hearing held on January 22, 1976, before the Senate Select Committee to Study Governmental Operations With Respect to Intelligence Activities. The hearing focuses on counterintelligence and features testimony from James Angleton and Scotty Miler. The discussion includes the organization of counterintelligence, its effectiveness, changes in approach over time, the need for a strong national counterintelligence focus, and potential threats from foreign intelligence services. The document also examines specific cases and procedures.
