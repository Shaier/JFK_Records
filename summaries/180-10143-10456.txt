This document consists of a collection of memos, cables, dispatches, reports, and telegrams primarily spanning the years 1960-1966. It details the activities, relationships, and perceptions surrounding Manuel Ray Rivero, a Cuban exile leader. The documents reveal concerns about Ray's ideology, trustworthiness, and effectiveness in the anti-Castro movement, alongside details of his attempts to gain support, establish organizations, and infiltrate Cuba. They also cover the involvement of U.S. agencies, internal conflicts within Ray's groups, and observations from various sources about Ray's character and capabilities.
