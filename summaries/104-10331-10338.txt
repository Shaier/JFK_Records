This document is a memorandum regarding the release of CIA employees' names in JFK records. It discusses the Agency's agreement to protect these names until May 1997, the challenges of collecting evidence to defend individual names, and concerns about the potential harm to national security from releasing a large number of names. It proposes revisiting the name issue with the ARRB, emphasizing the need to balance public interest with the Agency's security practices. It also analyzes memoranda concerning the treatment of CIA officer names in the JFK collection, focusing on criteria for postponing the release of names and the Directorate of Operations' steps for handling employee names in JFK records. The document includes memos and checklists reflecting completion of employee address searches.
