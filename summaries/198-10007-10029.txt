This document is a memorandum regarding a report on the CIA's use of radio broadcasting during the Cold War. It details a survey of radio broadcasting techniques, capabilities, targets, and problems. It recommends actions to improve CIA radio broadcasting, including:

*   Analyzing broadcasting and television systems for penetration opportunities.
*   Developing a plan for officer recruitment and training for covert broadcasting.
*   Exploring collaboration with the State Department to exploit intelligence sources.
*   Improving policy guidance dissemination.
*   Developing broadcasting devices for electronic intrusion.
*   Focusing on unconventional broadcasting techniques.

The report emphasizes the need to counter communist propaganda and exploit radio systems in friendly nations. It also highlights the importance of security, control, and personnel training for covert radio operations. It stresses the need for long-term planning and coordination between different agencies.
