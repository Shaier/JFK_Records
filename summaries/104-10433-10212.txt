This document is an intelligence report concerning Americans attending the World Peace Council (WPC) Conference in Budapest, Hungary, May 13-16, 1971. 

The report identifies specific Americans who spoke at committee meetings, and mentions that they were identified as members of the U.S. delegation. Also, mention is made that S. Faulkner served as president in a meeting of lawyer delegates, and that Albert Kahn translated a message from the widow of Rockwell Kent to the Conference. 

The report also mentions U.S. delegates Ruth Gage-Colby and Sylvia Kushner spoke at a meeting of Indochinese and American delegates, and that Herbert Aptheker and Carlton Goodlett were re-elected to the Presidential Board of the WPC.
