This document is a CIA information report, classified as Secret, concerning the alert status of Cuban diplomatic missions following the assassination of President Kennedy. The report, dated December 13, 1963, details how Cuban missions abroad were placed on high alert due to the Cuban government's fear of hostile actions. The missions were instructed to exercise extreme vigilance and maintain a 24-hour watch. The report also mentions an incident where a chief of mission was delayed in receiving permission to visit a friendly country. As of December 9, 1963, the alert order had not been revoked.
