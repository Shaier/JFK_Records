This document is a Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) report dated April 16, 1963, concerning the Cuban Revolutionary Council and its relationship with the United States, covering events from April 9, 1963, to April 15, 1963. The report details the potential resignation of Dr. Jose Miro Cardona, head of the Council, and the reasons behind it, which include disagreements with U.S. policy toward Cuba. The document also outlines the Council's position on Cuban participation in liberating Cuba, their continued fight against the Soviet-Castro regime, and their call for national unity among Cubans. In addition, it highlights the exiles' growing frustration and disillusionment with the U.S. government's perceived shift towards peaceful co-existence with Cuba, ultimately feeling abandoned by the U.S.
