This document is a transcript of a deposition of William Sturbitts, taken on April 16, 1975, before the Rockefeller Commission. The deposition covers a wide range of topics related to CIA activities, particularly concerning Cuba, anti-Castro efforts, and Cuban exiles. Key subjects include the organization and functions of the Miami Station, involvement of Cuban exiles in Agency operations, economic warfare against Cuba, and potential connections between the CIA and organized crime. The deposition also explores specific allegations, such as those related to assassination attempts on Fidel Castro and connections between the Agency and individuals like Frank Sturgis.
