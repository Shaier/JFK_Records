This document is an intelligence report from the CIA, dated August 14, 1962, concerning the views of the British Ambassador to South Vietnam, H.A.F. Hohler, on a neutralist solution for South Vietnam. The report details a conversation where Hohler reportedly expressed his belief that a neutralist coalition, combined with a third force in North Vietnam, was the only hope for Vietnam. It also includes commentary from other individuals, including Peter Roberts, an Information Officer at the British Embassy, and Frederick Nolting, the US Ambassador to South Vietnam, offering differing perspectives on Hohler's views and the potential for a neutralist solution. The document discusses concerns about a neutralist approach and potential communist takeover.
