This document contains information about an incident involving "John the Dee Jay" and a possible counter-intelligence interest. The source, Phillip W. May, was interviewed about an encounter at a bar in Datteln, Germany. John the Dee Jay made a general nuisance of himself, talked about security, and gave a list of telephone numbers he claimed were security information. He also asked about the Security Sergeant of the unit and persons formerly assigned to the unit. The source described John as being tall, solidly built, and wearing a furry coat and shoes. The document is an agent report dated February 4, 1972.
