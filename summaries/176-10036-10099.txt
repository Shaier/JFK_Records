This document is a Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) Information Report concerning the apprehensions of the Vietnamese Consul General in Singapore regarding recent events in Vietnam. The report, dated May 18, 1963, summarizes the Consul General's view that recent political acts may prove fatal to President Ngo Dinh Diem's regime. The Consul General cites the dismissal of Huynh Van Lang, the removal of Dr. Tran Kim Tuyen, and the Hue incident on Buddha's birthday as major factors in his assessment. He also notes that these actions have weakened the motivation and support for the regime among various segments of Vietnamese society, including loyal supporters, public servants, opposition leaders, intellectuals, and the non-Catholic peasantry.
