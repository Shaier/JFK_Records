This document is an addendum to a report summarizing a meeting held at the White House on March 28, 1975. The purpose of the meeting was to review the role of the White House in coordinating intelligence agency responses to the Senate Committee, ensuring uniformity in complying with information requests. The meeting participants agreed that access to raw files would generally not be granted to committee members or staff. They discussed sensitive items like informant identities and confidential expenditures. Electronic exploitation programs like Orkid, wiretaps, microphones, Telex, and electronic equipment were also discussed, noting the varying degrees of sensitivity involved. Other topics included past activities subject to newspaper publicity, such as the Anagram program, mail intercepts, and investigations related to the CP and Klan. Cointelpro and the Chilean project were mentioned, along with the exploitation of unavailable government records.
