This document is a collection of letters, testimony, statements, and other materials related to the CIA's involvement in the investigation of President John F. Kennedy's assassination. It includes correspondence with congressional committees, internal CIA memoranda, testimony from CIA officials and outside experts, and analysis of proposed legislation related to the release of assassination records. The documents cover topics such as the extent of CIA records related to the assassination, the process of declassifying and releasing these records, the challenges and controversies surrounding the effort, and the perspectives of various stakeholders. There are also records concerning Lee Harvey Oswald's activities, both before and after the assassination, and the CIA's efforts to work with other agencies in this effort.
