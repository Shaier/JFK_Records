This document is a transcript of a closed session of the Senate Select Committee to Study Governmental Operations with Respect to Intelligence Activities (the Church Committee) held on March 9, 1976. The committee members discuss various aspects of intelligence oversight, including the DCI's authority, budgetary control, covert actions, and the use of American citizens and companies in intelligence operations. They debate different options and express various concerns regarding the recommendations they are considering. The conversation also includes discussions about legislative authority, the proper balance of power between different government entities, and the need for accountability and oversight in intelligence activities.
