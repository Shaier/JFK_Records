This document is a memorandum from Dean Rusk to the President regarding an upcoming meeting with President Lopez Mateos of Mexico. It provides guidance on how to approach the meeting, emphasizing the importance of maintaining strong, friendly relations and addressing Mexico's sensitivities. The memorandum outlines key points of interest to both the United States and Mexico, including topics such as cooperation on Castro Communism, assistance on Panama, Red China, the Alliance for Progress, trade policies, and disputed tracts on the Rio Grande. It also anticipates topics Lopez Mateos is likely to raise, such as the salinity problem on the Lower Colorado River and the Bracero Program. The document is classified as secret and provides specific instructions on how to handle certain issues.
