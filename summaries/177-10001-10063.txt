This document is a CIA report from Uruguay dated November 24, 1963. It details the reaction of the Cuban embassy in Montevideo to the assassination of President Kennedy. The embassy requested extra police protection, fearing a mob attack. The police also provided extra protection to the Soviet legation. The report also notes that the populace of Montevideo was shocked and grief-stricken over President Kennedy's assassination, with a crowd of approximately 3000 people marching to the American embassy residence to express their sorrow. There had been no demonstrations against either the Cuban or Soviet diplomatic installations.
