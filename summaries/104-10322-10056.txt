This document is a memorandum from the Associate Deputy Director for Operations to the Director of Central Intelligence. It provides supplementary information about James Earl Ray, referencing a previous memorandum about background for a Rather interview. The memo discusses Dick Ober's recollections of discussions with the FBI regarding the Ray case, stating that actions were undertaken at the FBI's request. It also mentions a clipped outgoing cable concerning Eric Galt, the man initially identified as Dr. King's assassin, and requests addressees to determine if Galt had been in their area. The memo references a scribbled note about fingerprint proposals and the FBI providing fingerprint formulas and physical descriptions. Included is the a cable providing the description of suspect Galt.
