This document is a collection of dispatches, memoranda, and project outlines relating to Project LIFEAT, a technical surveillance operation run by the CIA in Mexico City. The documents cover the period from 1956 to 1963 and discuss renewals, amendments, budgets, objectives, personnel, operations, and evaluations of the project. The project involves telephone tapping and other surveillance activities against Soviet, satellite, and Cuban targets, as well as selected Mexican and American individuals and groups. The documentation emphasizes the project's role in providing operational support, counter-intelligence, and leads to other Station operations.
