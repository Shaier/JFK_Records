This document is a draft report from the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence Activities (SSCIA), also known as the Church Committee, dated September 8, 1975. It discusses the U.S. government's involvement in assassination plots against foreign leaders, specifically focusing on the cases of Fidel Castro, Patrice Lumumba, Rafael Trujillo, Ngo Dinh Diem, and General Rene Schneider. The report examines the authorization and control mechanisms surrounding these plots, highlighting issues such as the use of underworld figures, communication failures, and the interpretation of broad directives. It presents the Committee's findings and conclusions, condemning assassination as a tool of foreign policy and emphasizing the need for stricter oversight and ethical standards in covert operations. It also includes recommendations, calling for a statute making it a federal crime to commit or attempt an assassination.
