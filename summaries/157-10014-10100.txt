This document is a collection of notes and memoranda pertaining to Gordon Gray's role in the Eisenhower administration and his involvement in discussions related to covert operations, assassinations, Cuba, the Dominican Republic, and the Congo. It includes transcripts from the Rockefeller Commission testimony, Special Group Meeting minutes, and issues to be examined regarding Gray's testimony. The document focuses on the Eisenhower administration's attitudes towards Castro, Trujillo, and Lumumba. The records include references to Gray's contact with President Eisenhower and other key officials, particularly in relation to special operations, planning, sabotage, and covert activities in various countries.
