This document is an interview summary from August 22, 1975, with Victor Hedgeman, conducted by Bill Bader, regarding "The Congo." It discusses Hedgeman's background, his involvement in the Congo as the first Chief of Station for the Agency, and U.S. policy in the Congo. It mentions figures like Lumumba, Mobutu, Dulles, and Timberlake, and touches on topics such as financial support for Congolese politicians, efforts to remove Lumumba from power, and the paramilitary operations that followed. Hedgeman contends the coup of Mobutu was supported and managed by the CIA. It also mentions his relationship with Mobutu, and concludes by recommending CIA employees who might testify well on the Congo issue.
