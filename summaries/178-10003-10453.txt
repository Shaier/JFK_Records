This document is a letter from Senator Frank Church, Chairman of the Senate Select Committee to study governmental operations with respect to intelligence activities, to William Colby, Director of the Central Intelligence Agency. It outlines the committee's plans for examining CIA's foreign intelligence activities and covert actions, particularly those involving domestic activities. The letter requests a wide range of documents and personnel for review, covering topics like counterintelligence, domestic operations supporting foreign activities, relationships with other organizations, and misuse of CIA resources. It also poses questions about the legal authority, limitations, purpose, and control mechanisms governing CIA activities. The letter includes attachments listing specific documents and individuals the committee wants to examine.
