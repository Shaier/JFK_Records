This 1977 CIA memorandum discusses Tadeusz (Tad) Witold Szulc, a journalist, focusing on long-held suspicions of his potential connections to hostile foreign intelligence. It details a history of concerns about his activities, from his early career to his later writings critical of the CIA and the US government. The document outlines specific incidents and reports that raised suspicion, including alleged contacts with Communist figures and a possible role in spreading disinformation. It also highlights that despite the concerns, there is no conclusive evidence to prove that he is a foreign agent. However, his later activities align with what benefits the Soviets.
