This document is a CIA information telegram dated December 6, 1963, containing observations from Soviet bloc and Yugoslav diplomats in Athens regarding the assassination of President Kennedy and its potential impact on East-West relations. The telegram includes reports of comments made by a Yugoslav ambassador, a Czech first secretary, a Soviet first secretary, and a Romanian ambassador. They expressed concerns about the potential for delayed improvement in East-West relations, changes in US foreign policy, and the influence of right-wing elements in America. The document is classified as SECRET and NO FOREIGN DISSEM. It also notes that missing portions of the report will only be disseminated if they materially affect the sense of the report.
