This document is a report from James Angleton to the Rockefeller Commission regarding CIA activities within the U.S., dated 1975. It outlines a critique of the counterintelligence function within the Agency, discusses its history, identifies current weaknesses, and makes recommendations for improvement. The report emphasizes the importance of counterintelligence for national security, highlighting concerns about Soviet deception and penetration. It expresses concerns about the current state of counterintelligence within the CIA, advocating for more resources, better organization, improved training, and greater interagency cooperation. It also mentions specific cases like the Nosenko defector situation and the need for a centralized authority to deal with deception.
