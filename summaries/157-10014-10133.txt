This is a transcript of a hearing held before the Senate Select Committee to Study Governmental Operations With Respect to Intelligence Activities on March 25, 1976. The committee discusses recommendations regarding the CIA's involvement with academics and the media, including the recruitment of foreign students, the subsidizing of publications, and the use of journalists for operational purposes. Senators debate the merits of these recommendations, with concerns raised about the potential impact on cultural exchange programs and the need to balance intelligence gathering with the protection of individual rights and academic freedom. The committee takes votes on several recommendations, with some disagreement and reservations expressed.
