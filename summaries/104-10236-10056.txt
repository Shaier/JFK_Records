This document is a memorandum for the record detailing a meeting between the author and Dr. Miro Cardona on October 6, 1961. It outlines Miro Cardona's proposals for anti-Castro activities, including propaganda campaigns targeting Cuba, Miami, and Latin America. It also discusses a commando plan for clandestine operations in Cuba, requesting U.S. support in the form of weapons, equipment, and funds. Furthermore, the document mentions Miro's views on the Argentine rupture of relations with Cuba, his plans to attend events in New York City, his perspective on the Propaganda Commission, and his opinions on various Cuban political figures and groups. The memo also touches on Miro's concerns about the need for a unified anti-Castro resistance and his desire for a small office to coordinate activities.
