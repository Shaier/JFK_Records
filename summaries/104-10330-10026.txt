This document is a memorandum from the Deputy Director for Operations of the CIA to the Chairman of the JFK Assassination Records Review Board regarding the potential release of JFK documents. It expresses concerns about the release of information that would identify CIA stations, citing potential political embarrassment to host governments, threats to sensitive operations and liaison relationships, and damage to national security. The memo specifically urges against releasing information about stations in the Congo and Senegal, and details the potential negative consequences of revealing stations in various European, Asian, and North African countries. It also emphasizes the general damage done by any disclosure of operational presence, which could weaken confidence in the CIA's ability to protect sources and methods.
