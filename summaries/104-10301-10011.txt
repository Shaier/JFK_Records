This document is a historical overview of the Special Investigations Group (CI/SIG) within the CIA's Counterintelligence (CI) Staff, focusing on its functions, personnel, and activities from the mid-1950s to the late 1970s. It covers topics such as the group's expansion of responsibilities, its role in investigating potential security leaks and hostile penetrations, its involvement in the HONETOL investigations, and its management of defectors and sensitive information. It also discusses the challenges the CI Staff faced in dealing with a massive accumulation of files and records, and efforts to integrate them into the agency's central system following the Rockefeller Commission and congressional inquiries.
