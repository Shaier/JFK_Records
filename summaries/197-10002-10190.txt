This document is a Central Intelligence Agency report, dated November 20-26, 1963. It summarizes military activities and highlights from the German Service Military, focusing on the Soviet bloc in East Germany. It notes the absence of indications of preparation for attack by Soviet bloc forces. The report mentions precautionary measures of a general nature by Soviet and satellite forces following the assassination of President Kennedy, including special alert measures ordered in the Oranienburg Garrison. It also mentions normal training activities in the Soviet bloc armed forces, and control posts staggered in depths up to 1 kilometer from the interzonal border during weekend nights, and that one F-class submarine passed northward through the Great Belt.
