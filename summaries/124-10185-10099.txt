This document contains memorandums and reports from the CIA discussing various sensitive activities, including support for the US Secret Service for national conventions, surveillance of US citizens and foreign governments, domestic activities performed by various CIA offices and divisions, use of informants, and cooperation with other government agencies like the FBI, DoD, and Justice Department, as well as other programs, many relating to domestic intelligence gathering. The reports express concerns about some of these activities being improper or exceeding the CIA's charter and highlight the need for operational security and justification for actions taken. The documents span a period from 1972 to 1975.
