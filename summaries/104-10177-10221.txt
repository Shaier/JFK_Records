This document consists of a collection of official dispatches, routing sheets, memoranda, and a personal record form primarily relating to John Augustus McVickar Jr., a sales representative for Trans World Airline (TWA) in Paris, France, and his potential recruitment by the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA). The documents span from November 1949 to February 1950 and involve communications between various CIA stations, primarily Paris and Washington. The dispatches describe McVickar’s personal and professional background, financial situation, and network of contacts, as well as assessments of his suitability for intelligence work. The records explore the possibility of leveraging McVickar’s position at TWA to gather information and to potentially recruit other agents.
