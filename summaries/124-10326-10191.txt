This FBI document details information gathered on March 20, 1962, regarding anti-Castro activities related to Cuba. The information comes from Michael J.P. Malone, Vice President of Czarnikow-Rionda Company, who is also employed by the King Ranch of Kingsville, Texas. Malone had accompanied Robert Kleberg of the King Ranch to Washington, DC, on March 15, 1962, for a meeting with Vice President Lyndon B. Johnson. Malone didn't attend the meeting, but was advised by Kleberg as to what happened at the meeting. The conversation revolved around potential US action in Cuba, with conflicting information regarding plans from different sources including the Vice President, CIA contacts, and other Washington individuals. The document also mentions the Cuban Revolutionary Council's instability and Dr. Jose Miro Cardona's limited influence.
