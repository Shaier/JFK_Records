This document contains a collection of neighborhood checks conducted in November 1968 regarding Manuel Marti, a Private in the U.S. Army. Individuals living near Marti's former residence in the Bronx, New York, were interviewed to gather information about his character, background, and loyalty to the United States. The reports generally portray Marti as a quiet, respectable individual from a good family, with no known adverse interactions with law enforcement or questionable political affiliations. Interviewees suggest that Marti be favorably considered for a position of trust and responsibility within the U.S. Government.
