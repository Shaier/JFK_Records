This document is a communication from the CIA to the FBI, dated December 14, 1973, concerning telephone taps on calls initiated in the USA to a Cuban intelligence officer in Mexico City. It discusses an individual named Orlando Serrano who made calls to the Cuban Embassy in Mexico City using double-talk and speaking in Spanish. The document identifies individuals possibly connected to Serrano and their roles, including Manuel Rodriguez and Enrique Cicardlabrada. It also mentions the possibility that Serrano is identical to Emilio Echarte Pedroso. The document includes details about the telephone calls, such as locations and the content of the conversations. The CIA is forwarding a tape recording of one of the conversations to the FBI for review. The document also notes that the caller used Cuban Spanish.
