This document is a memorandum for the record detailing a requirements briefing for an FBI informant preparing to travel to Cuba. The informant, Victor Thomas Vicente, has been working undercover for the FBI, penetrating pro-Castro organizations in New York City. The memo outlines the arrangements for his trip to Cuba, including the assistance of the Cuban Ambassador to the UN in obtaining a visa and travel arrangements. It also details the key information the informant should gather while in Cuba, focusing on Cuban-Soviet relations, the demobilization of the militia, missile information, and the state of communism on the island. The memo emphasizes the importance of security considerations and highlights the cooperation between the FBI and the Agency in this operation.
