This document is a witness statement from Victor Lee Trinkala, taken on February 19, 1970, as part of the JFK Assassination System investigation. Trinkala, a discharged Army private, is questioned about his conscientious objector status, his acquaintance with Richard Nagel and Tony Muneer, his military service, and possible connections to dissident groups. He affirms his support for the US Constitution but expresses reservations about fighting in Vietnam. He also states that he supports killing in self-defense, if it is absolutely necessary. The statement concludes with an affidavit signed by Trinkala, attesting to the truthfulness of the content.
