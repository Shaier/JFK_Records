This document contains information about Henryk Ignacy Janikowski, who was of interest to the Central Intelligence Agency. The document details a connection between Janikowski and a man named Erik Schott, an architect in West Germany. Schott was interrogated by the West German service and provided information about Janikowski's activities involving buying and shipping cars to Poland using dollars obtained on the black market. The document also mentions a connection between Janikowski and an employee of the Mexican Embassy in Warsaw, who was sending money to Schott for deposit in his bank account. According to the West German service, there was no evidence indicating that Schott was involved in intelligence activities.
