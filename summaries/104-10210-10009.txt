This document is a Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) study analyzing the information provided by Soviet defector Yuriy Ivanovich Nosenko regarding his bona fides and potential KGB dispatch. It details the methodology used, including interviews, polygraph tests, and collaboration with the FBI, to assess the consistency and credibility of Nosenko's claims about his life, KGB career, motivations, and knowledge of KGB operations.  The study presents analytical comments concerning Nosenko's credibility and provides annexes with related information, such as statements from Soviet officials, summaries of cases, and the Cherepanov Papers. The document aims to determine whether Nosenko was genuinely defecting or a dispatched agent, while considering the limitations and potential biases in the available information.
