This FBI document, dated June 23, 1964, relays information from a CIA contact in London regarding a conversation intercepted by British intelligence. The conversation, which took place on May 8, 1964, involved a Cuban Embassy counselor in London and an unidentified male visitor. The topic was the assassination of President John F. Kennedy. The visitor inquired about a man named "Foster" and his possible presence in Cuba. The Cuban official denied that "Foster" had been to Cuba and mentioned that the man had been denied a visa to Cuba. The official also made comments about the assassination being an act of imperialism. The document suggests the individual referred to as "Foster" may have been Lee Harvey Oswald.
