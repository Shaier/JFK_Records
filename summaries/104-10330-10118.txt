This document is a memorandum from J. Barry Harrelson, JFK Project Officer, to the Executive Director of the Assassination Records Review Board, dated May 13, 1998. It outlines the CIA's proposal on alias documentation and accommodation addresses in assassination records. It discusses the potential damage to national security, diplomatic relations, and intelligence operations that could result from the disclosure of specific information related to CIA aliases and accommodation addresses used by officers and assets. The memorandum recommends that the release of such information be postponed and that generic substitute language be used instead to avoid compromising ongoing operations, endangering individuals, and jeopardizing relationships with foreign governments and private sector firms. It details the types of alias documentation, the reasons for their use, and the potential consequences of their disclosure, including damage to diplomatic relations, litigation, and physical danger to CIA officers and assets.
