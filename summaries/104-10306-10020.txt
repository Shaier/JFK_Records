This document is a collection of US government memoranda and correspondence, primarily from 1962-1964, concerning CIA activities and US foreign policy, specifically related to Cuba, Vietnam, and general Communist threats. It includes directives from President Kennedy related to Operation Mongoose and preventing provocative actions against Cuba, concerns about publicity surrounding covert operations and prisoner exchanges, assessments of Castro's potential in the Western Hemisphere, and discussions of clandestine channels to Communist countries. The documents also discuss CIA activities in South Vietnam, including reporting on the Diem regime and its effectiveness, and differences of opinion within the American community regarding the situation there.
