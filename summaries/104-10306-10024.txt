This document contains memoranda and meeting minutes from the Special Group, a committee responsible for overseeing sensitive U.S. foreign policy initiatives, primarily concerning covert operations related to Cuba, reconnaissance activities in various regions (including the Soviet Union, China, and Southeast Asia), and other covert actions in countries like Bolivia, Congo, and Italy. Discussions cover topics such as agent infiltration, sabotage, propaganda, dealing with exile groups, reconnaissance flights, and concerns related to Soviet influence and activities. The documents also include information on covert programs, budgets, and intelligence gathering.
