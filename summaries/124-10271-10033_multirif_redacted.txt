This document is a collection of reports, memos, and correspondence primarily from June and July 1975, pertaining to the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence Activities' (SSC) investigation into the FBI and related areas. The documents cover topics such as the Cabinet Committee to Combat Terrorism, FBI's activities (including COINTELPRO, Huston Plan, surveillance of Martin Luther King Jr.), and intelligence collection methods, as well as responses to SSC requests for information and testimony. There is also a focus on the clearance and authorization processes for these activities, and coordination with other government agencies. The documents also discuss various individuals involved, including staff members and former FBI and CIA employees, and their testimonies or potential testimonies before the SSC.
