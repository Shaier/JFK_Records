This document is a set of directives for Cuban intelligence representatives in Haiti, dated February 4, 1965. It outlines priority and important areas of focus for intelligence gathering, including plans of aggression against Cuba, progressive political forces, attitudes of political parties toward the Cuban Revolution, the solidarity movement with Cuba, strategic information, the Communist Party of Haiti, relations with the United States, internal politics and social situation in Haiti, the armed forces, the church, and members of the foreign service. The directives also include information on counterrevolutionary operations, the national economy, and the governmental structure of Haiti.
