This document is a memorandum discussing Clemard Joseph Charles, a banker from Haiti, who had recently arrived in New York. It details communications and actions taken by various U.S. government agencies (Army, CIA) in response to Charles's arrival and his stated desire to meet with high-ranking officials, including the President and Vice President. The memo outlines efforts to gather information on Charles, coordinate meetings, and resolve potential conflicts between agencies involved. Concerns about Charles's background, political affiliations, and the U.S. government's potential interactions with him are highlighted, leading to a decision to disengage from further contact.
