This document is a memorandum for the record discussing a meeting between a CIA staff member and W. David Slawson of the Warren Commission staff, about inconsistencies in information provided by the CIA and State Department. The discussion focuses on Soviet emigration policies for citizens married to foreigners, specifically regarding the OSWALDs' departure from the USSR. The memo details the CIA's basis for their statements and addresses Slawson's concerns about resolving inconsistencies. The document concludes with a listing of cases involving Soviets married to foreigners, noting details such as visa processing times and whether the couples left the USSR together. There are also notes included about specific cases and possible intelligence connections, including a list of Soviet women married to other nationals.
